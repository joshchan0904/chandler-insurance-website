Minnesota Summer Storms & Hail Damage – Is Your Home Protected?

Published May 13, 2026 • 6 min read • By Chandler Insurance Agency

Every summer, Minnesota homeowners get a harsh reminder: our storms are no joke.

From golf-ball sized hail in the Twin Cities to straight-line winds across the Iron Range, summer weather can cause thousands of dollars in damage in just minutes.

What Summer Storms Typically Damage

  • Roofs – Hail can shatter shingles and cause leaks
  • Siding & Windows – High winds and flying debris
  • Vehicles – Dented cars and cracked windshields (comprehensive coverage helps)
  • Outdoor Structures – Sheds, decks, fences, and playground equipment
Important: Standard homeowners policies usually cover sudden storm damage, but wear and tear or lack of maintenance is not covered. Make sure your roof isn’t already 15+ years old.

How to Prepare Before the Next Storm

  1. Have your roof inspected every 3–5 years
  2. Trim trees that could fall on your house
  3. Secure outdoor furniture and grills
  4. Know your deductible (many people choose higher deductibles to save money — but make sure you can afford it after a storm)

Does Your Policy Cover Hail?

Most standard homeowners policies in Minnesota do cover hail damage under the “wind and hail” peril. However, some older policies or policies with “named storm” exclusions may limit coverage.
