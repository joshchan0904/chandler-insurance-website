Boat & PWC Insurance in Minnesota: What You Need for Summer 2026

Published May 13, 2026 • 7 min read • By Chandler Insurance Agency

Summer in Minnesota means one thing for thousands of families: **time on the water**.

Whether you own a fishing boat, pontoon, speedboat, or jet ski (PWC), having the right insurance is just as important as having a life jacket. Minnesota’s short but intense boating season, combined with unpredictable summer storms, creates risks that many boaters underestimate until it’s too late.

What Does Boat Insurance Typically Cover?

  • Physical Damage – Collision, theft, vandalism, fire, and storm damage (including hail and high winds)
  • Liability – If you injure someone or damage another boat or dock
  • Personal Effects – Coverage for fishing gear, electronics, life jackets, and belongings on board
  • Medical Payments – For injuries to you and your passengers while on the water
  • Towing & Assistance – On-water towing, fuel delivery, and breakdown help (very useful on remote lakes)
Important: Most homeowners policies have very limited or no coverage for boats and PWCs. You almost always need a separate policy, especially if your boat has significant value.

Why Minnesota Boaters Need Specialized Coverage

Minnesota lakes present unique risks that standard policies often don’t address well — especially for boaters in Grand Rapids & Itasca County and Virginia & the Iron Range:

  • Short but intense boating season (Memorial Day to Labor Day)
  • Sudden summer storms and high winds that can appear with little warning
  • Heavy recreational traffic on popular lakes like Mille Lacs, Leech Lake, and Lake Vermilion
  • Long winter storage periods (need proper winterization and storage coverage)
  • Remote lakes where getting help can be difficult and expensive

How Much Does Boat Insurance Cost in Minnesota?

Most Minnesota boat owners pay between $250 – $650 per year for comprehensive coverage, depending on:

  • Boat value and type (pontoons are often cheaper to insure than high-performance boats)
  • Engine horsepower
  • How you use the boat (pleasure, fishing, watersports, or commercial)
  • Your boating experience and claims history
  • Where you store the boat in the off-season
