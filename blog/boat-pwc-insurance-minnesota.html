Boat & PWC Insurance in Minnesota: What You Need for Summer 2026

Published May 13, 2026 • 7 min read • By Chandler Insurance Agency

Summer in Minnesota means one thing for thousands of families: **time on the water**.

Whether you own a fishing boat, pontoon, speedboat, or jet ski (PWC), having the right insurance is just as important as having a life jacket.

What Does Boat Insurance Typically Cover?

  • Physical Damage – Collision, theft, vandalism, fire, and storm damage
  • Liability – If you injure someone or damage another boat
  • Personal Effects – Coverage for gear, electronics, and belongings on board
  • Medical Payments – For injuries to you and your passengers
  • Towing & Assistance – On-water towing and breakdown help
Important: Most homeowners policies have very limited or no coverage for boats and PWCs. You almost always need a separate policy.

Why Minnesota Boaters Need Specialized Coverage

Minnesota lakes present unique risks:

  • Short but intense boating season (Memorial Day to Labor Day)
  • Sudden summer storms and high winds
  • Heavy recreational traffic on popular lakes
  • Long winter storage periods (need proper winterization coverage)

How Much Does Boat Insurance Cost in Minnesota?

Most Minnesota boat owners pay between $250 – $650 per year for comprehensive coverage, depending on:

  • Boat value and type
  • Engine horsepower
  • How you use the boat (pleasure, fishing, watersports)
  • Your boating experience and claims history
