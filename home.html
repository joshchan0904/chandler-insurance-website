Home & Cabin Insurance

Protecting your biggest investment in Minnesota

What is Home & Cabin Insurance?

Home and cabin insurance protects your property, belongings, and liability in case of damage, theft, or accidents. In Minnesota, where harsh winters, heavy snow, and ice dams are common, having the right coverage is essential. Whether you live in Grand Rapids, Virginia, or own a lake cabin in Itasca County, our policies are designed specifically for the unique risks homeowners and cabin owners face in this region.

How does Home & Cabin Insurance work?

Home insurance works by protecting your dwelling, personal property, and liability. If your home or cabin is damaged by a covered peril (such as fire, windstorm, hail, or ice dam), you file a claim, pay your deductible, and the insurance company helps cover the rest. We partner with top-rated carriers to offer customized policies for year-round homes and seasonal cabins across Minnesota.

Why is Home & Cabin Insurance important in Minnesota?

Minnesota homeowners face unique risks including heavy snowfall, ice dams, frozen pipes, and severe storms. Without proper coverage, you could face thousands of dollars in out-of-pocket expenses. Our policies include critical protections like ice dam coverage, water backup, and liability for cabin guests — all tailored for the Iron Range and surrounding areas.

What coverages are included?

  • Dwelling Coverage: Protects the structure of your home or cabin
  • Personal Property: Covers furniture, electronics, clothing, and other belongings
  • Liability Protection: Covers legal and medical costs if someone is injured on your property
  • Ice Dam & Water Backup: Essential coverage for Minnesota winters
  • Additional Living Expenses: Pays for hotel and meals if you can't live in your home
  • Optional Coverages: Scheduled personal property, identity theft, and equipment breakdown

FAQs about Home & Cabin Insurance

Does home insurance cover ice dams?

Yes — most policies in Minnesota include ice dam coverage, which pays to remove ice dams and repair resulting water damage. This is one of the most important coverages for Minnesota homeowners.

How much does home insurance cost in Minnesota?

The average cost ranges from $1,800–$2,600 per year depending on home value, location, construction type, and coverage limits. Cabins may cost slightly less but often require higher deductibles.

Can I insure my cabin if I only use it seasonally?

Yes. We offer seasonal cabin policies with options for higher deductibles and lower premiums when the property is unoccupied for part of the year.

Does home insurance cover theft?

Yes, theft of personal property is typically covered. We recommend scheduling high-value items like jewelry, firearms, or electronics for extra protection.

Can I bundle home and auto insurance?

Absolutely — bundling usually saves 10–25% on both policies and makes managing coverage much easier.
