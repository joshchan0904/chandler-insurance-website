What is Workers' Compensation Insurance?

Workers' compensation (workers' comp) provides medical benefits and wage replacement to employees who are injured or become ill while working. In Minnesota, most businesses with employees are required to carry workers' comp coverage. It protects both your workers and your business from the high costs of workplace injuries.

Who Needs Workers' Comp in Northern Minnesota?

Almost every business in Grand Rapids, Itasca County, and the Iron Range with employees needs workers' compensation. This includes:

  • Construction contractors, roofers, and excavators
  • Manufacturing and wood product businesses
  • Logging, forestry, and mining support companies
  • Retail stores, restaurants, and hospitality businesses
  • Healthcare clinics, dental offices, and professional services
  • Landscaping, snow removal, and property maintenance companies

Frequently Asked Questions

Is workers' comp required in Minnesota?

Yes. Most employers with employees in Minnesota must carry workers' compensation insurance. There are limited exceptions for very small businesses and certain independent contractors.

How is workers' comp priced?

Premiums are based on your payroll, job classifications (class codes), claims history, and safety programs. We help you classify employees correctly to keep costs down.

Do I need workers' comp if I use subcontractors?

Often yes. In Minnesota, you can still be responsible for injuries to uninsured subcontractors. We can help you understand when you need coverage for subs.
