Frequently Asked Questions — Contractors Insurance in Minnesota

Do I need workers’ comp if I’m a sole proprietor or have only subcontractors?

In Minnesota, sole proprietors and single-member LLCs are generally not required to carry workers’ comp on themselves, but many general contractors and project owners require proof of coverage before you can work on their jobs. We can help structure the right solution.

What’s the difference between general liability and a BOP for contractors?

General Liability covers third-party claims. A BOP bundles liability with property coverage for your tools, materials, and shop. Many smaller contractors start with a BOP and add scheduled tools/equipment or commercial auto as needed.

Will my policy cover work I do in the winter (snow removal, ice management)?

Yes — but you must tell us. Snow and ice management often requires specific endorsements or higher limits because of slip-and-fall exposure. We make sure your policy actually covers the work you perform.

How fast can I get a quote?

Most contractor quotes are ready within 24–48 hours after we receive your information. Complex risks (large subs, high-risk work, or multiple states) may take a bit longer, but we move quickly.
